## Conference Publications

### Towards EMG-Driven Pseudo-Haptic Feedback for VR Surgical Training

Lan, W. et al.  
IEEE Conference on Virtual Reality and 3D User Interfaces Workshops (VRW), 2026.

This work explores how surface EMG can drive effort-adaptive pseudo-haptic feedback for VR surgical training tasks. The system estimates muscular effort in real time and modulates multimodal feedback to guide users toward optimal force application.

## Posters

### Qìfield: Externalizing Qi as a Palmar Haptic Field for Volitional Extension in Tai Chi Practices

Lan, W. et al.  
CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI EA), 2026.

The project introduces **Volitional Extension**, a design lens for understanding how intention propagates through bodily movement. The VR Tai Chi system renders “Qi flow” using palm-centered airflow and vibrotactile feedback modulated by EMG.
