My research explores how embodied intention can be sensed, interpreted, and augmented through multimodal interactive systems. I am particularly interested in hand-centric interaction, haptic rendering, physiological computing, and XR-based embodied media.

## Research Theme 1: Hand-Centric Multimodal Feedback in XR
This line of work investigates how hand and palm-centered feedback can support movement understanding, spatial force perception, and embodied interaction in virtual environments.

Related projects:
- VR Tai Chi “Qi” Augmentation
- Palm-based multimodal haptic rendering
- Gesture-driven XR interaction systems

## Research Theme 2: Bioactuated Embodied Interaction
This theme focuses on systems that couple intention sensing and bodily stimulation. I use EMS/EMG-based actuation to explore traction, impact, resistance, and force-like feedback in interactive experiences.

Related projects:
- Spider-EMS Interaction System
- EMG/EMS-based action feedback
- Bioactuated XR mechanics

## Research Theme 3: Biosignal-Driven Adaptive Interfaces
I build real-time pipelines that transform physiological signals into interaction control and adaptive feedback. This includes signal processing, feature extraction, classification, and interactive system integration.

Related projects:
- Real-time BMI pipeline development
- EMG/EEG-based interaction control
- Adaptive haptic rendering systems
